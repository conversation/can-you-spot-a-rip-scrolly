### To survive a rip current:

1. **Stay calm**: Avoid panic and conserve energy.
2. **Don‚Äôt fight the current**: Swim parallel to the shore to escape the narrow flow.
3. **Signal for help**: Raise an arm to alert lifeguards or bystanders.
4. **Float if needed**: If unable to swim, float on your back to conserve energy until help arrives.

## Prevention and Awareness

Rip currents are most common during periods of high surf and can occur on any beach with breaking waves. Checking local weather forecasts, obeying beach warning flags, and swimming near lifeguards can significantly reduce the risk.

Educating the public about rip currents is essential to saving lives. Beaches should provide clear signage, and swimmers must prioritize vigilance and caution in the ocean environment.
